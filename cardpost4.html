The Villa by Rachel Hawkins
A girls' trip to an Italian holiday home isn't as peaceful as it seems... When Emily starts looking into the property's history, she finds that in 1974, an up-and-coming musician died right there in the villa. Past secrets and present betrayals are unveiled, as is another potential death.

Spare by Prince Harry
Arguably one of the most anticipated titles of 2023 is Spare, the tell-all autobiography by Prince Harry, The Duke of Sussex (ghostwritten by the Pulitzer prize-winning writer JR Moehringer). Readers can expect insights into his childhood, thrown upside down by his mother Princess Diana's untimely death, as well as his musings on life, love and public scrutiny.

The Fun Habit by Mike Rucker
While we continue to search for the cure to burnout and stress, psychologist and behavioural scientist Dr. Mike Rucker is having fun. The Fun Habit is your helpful guide to unleashing the potential that genuine fun can provide.

Funny Ethnics by Shirley Le This debut novel by Shirley Le is a humourous story about a Vietnamese-Australian woman growing up in Western Sydney. In this book, Shirley navigates racism and identity in Australia with wit and blunt observation.
